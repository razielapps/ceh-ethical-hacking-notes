Topic 53: Introduction to DOS and DDOS

A Denial-of-Service (DoS) attack is a cyber-attack aimed at disrupting the normal functioning of a targeted system, such as a website, server, or network, by overwhelming it with a flood of illegitimate traffic or resource requests. The goal is not to steal data, but to make the service unavailable to its legitimate users.

Think of it like this: A DoS attack is like a single person repeatedly calling a pizza shop, placing fake orders and asking endless questions. This ties up the phone line, preventing legitimate customers from getting through to place real orders.

A Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack is a much larger-scale version of this. Instead of one attacker, a DDoS attack uses a vast network of compromised computers, known as a **botnet**, to launch the assault simultaneously. This is like thousands of people, all coordinated, calling the same pizza shop at the exact same time. The volume of calls is so immense that it completely shuts down the phone system.

**Key Differences:**

- **DoS (Denial-of-Service):** Originates from a single machine or a small number of machines. Easier to mitigate by simply blocking the source IP address.
- **DDoS (Distributed Denial-of-Service):** Originates from thousands or even millions of machines distributed globally. Much harder to mitigate because the traffic comes from everywhere at once, mimicking legitimate users.

**The Motivations Behind DoS/DDoS Attacks:**

- **Extortion:** Attackers threaten to take a company's website offline unless a ransom is paid.
- **Hacktivism:** Attacking a website for political or ideological reasons.
- **Competitive Sabotage:** A business targeting a competitor's online services.
- **A Smokescreen:** Launching a DDoS attack to distract the security team while a more subtle data breach is carried out.

For an ethical hacker, understanding these attacks is crucial. You may be hired to test an organization's resilience against them (a "stress test") or to help design defenses. It is critical to note that launching a real DoS/DDoS attack against any target without explicit written authorization is illegal and highly unethical.

Glossary for Topic 53:

Denial-of-Service (DoS): An attack that aims to shut down a machine or network, making it inaccessible to its intended users.

Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS): A DoS attack that originates from many distributed sources, such as a botnet.

Botnet: A network of private computers infected with malicious software and controlled as a group without the owners' knowledge, e.g., to launch DDoS attacks.

Bandwidth Consumption: A type of DDoS attack that aims to consume all of the target's available network bandwidth.

Resource Depletion: A type of DDoS attack that aims to exhaust the target's system resources, such as CPU, memory, or connection state tables.

Stress Test: An authorized simulation of a DoS/DDoS attack to evaluate a system's capacity and resilience.

Mitigation: The steps taken to reduce the severity or impact of an attack.
